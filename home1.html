Broken Torsion Springs

Danger - High Tension

Your garage door torsion springs balance the weight of the door. When a spring snaps (often accompanied by a loud bang), the door loses all support, making it dangerous or impossible to open.

Recommended Safety Steps:

  • Do Not Attempt to Lift: Trying to open a door manually or with an opener when a spring is broken can burn out the motor or cause serious injury.
  • Clear the Area: Keep children and pets away from the door under tension.
  • Replace in Pairs: Since both springs experience identical wear, we recommend replacing both simultaneously to prevent a second failure soon after.

Off-Track & Crooked Rollers

Warning - High Risk

If the door becomes crooked or jumps its tracks, it indicates that a roller has popped out or a lift cable has snapped. Attempting to force the door closed can bend the track or cause the door to fall.

Recommended Safety Steps:

  • Stop Operation: Do not activate the automatic opener. This will prevent twisting the tracks further.
  • Visually Inspect: Check if any rollers are fully out of the track channels.
  • Call for Realignment: Professional tools are required to reset the heavy cables under tension and re-align the tracking frames safely.
Book Track Realignment

Opener Motor Malfunction

Warning - Moderate Risk

When the motor hums but the door fails to move, the internal nylon gears may be stripped, or the travel limits need recalibration. If the opener is completely unresponsive, check the power source and remote batteries first.

Recommended Safety Steps:

  • Check Remote & Sensors: Ensure the safety eye-sensors at the bottom of the tracks are aligned and the lenses are clean.
  • Use Emergency Release: Pull the red emergency cord to disconnect the carriage. Test if the door lifts easily by hand to check for mechanical blockages.
  • Upgrade to Smart Opener: If your opener is over 15 years old, consider upgrading to a modern belt-drive unit with WiFi and backup battery capabilities.
Book Motor Diagnosis

Noisy Squeaks & Grinding

Low Risk - Maintenance Alert

Loud squeaks, squeals, or grinding sounds occur when moving steel components lack proper lubrication or when rollers are worn down. Ignoring these sounds puts excessive strain on the garage opener motor.

Recommended Safety Steps:

  • Do Not Use WD-40: Avoid standard degreasers. Use specialized silicone or lithium-based garage door lubricants on hinges, rollers, and springs.
  • Check Roller Wear: If you have steel rollers, upgrading to nylon rollers will reduce operating noise by up to 75%.
  • Schedule a Tune-Up: A full 20-point inspection will tighten loose hinges, balance the spring tension, and ensure quiet, smooth travel.
